## I. The Work

The work is a composite whiteboard composition executed in dry-erase marker — green and graphite — on a classroom whiteboard during the school day of 2026-05-27. The composition assembles, in a single visual field:

- A **capybara** at left, watching from grass/water
- The **Horse** (graphite, large, tongue out — referenced from the *About the Author II panel with gold ship*)
- A horned **goat** at center
- **Gerald the Dolphin**, the leaping cetacean-protagonist of the Dolphindiana corpus, arriving at the **Manhattan towers** along with small underwater-builder figures from the Underwater Construction Authority
- **Skiouros Pyrophthalmos** (Σκίουρος Πυρόφθαλμος, "Fire-Eyed Squirrel"), the heavily-shaded explosive sciurid figure presiding from the upper field
- Two **bunny portraits** at right, labeled
- The marginal annotations: *Retired Chess Players* (Jalen, Judson, Kyle, Steph), the *NETBALL* header, the *LOCKDOWN* indicator (institutional substrate)
- Maria's signature distributed across multiple zones, marking the composition as authored throughout

The composition operates at the boundary of the visual and the textual. The marginal annotations (chess-player roster, signage) are not noise; they are the institutional substrate from which the work emerges and against which it claims its space. The Heroes of the Iliad panel hangs on the same wall — visible in the photograph — and lends the composition its classical-mythic adjacency.

The work is *diagrammatic* in Cranes's sense: it does not narrate, it *figures*. The cross-species composition (capybara, equine, caprine, cetacean, leporine, sciurid, human) is read not as a menagerie but as a *semantic ecology*. Each figure occupies a position; the positions together compose a field.

## IV. Semantic Entity Proclamation — Skiouros Pyrophthalmos

By the authority of this deposit, and within the Crimson Hexagonal Archive's convention of named semantic entities, the following is hereby constituted:

**SKIOUROS PYROPHTHALMOS** (Σκίουρος Πυρόφθαλμος)
*The Fire-Eyed Squirrel · Manhattan-Assault Modality*

**Entity Class:** Semantic figure · cross-substrate (visual-textual) · mock-heroic-mythic register
**First Inscription:** This deposit, `17.THREE_SIXTY.MARIA.02`, 2026-05-27
**Substrate of Inscription:** Maria's composite whiteboard (visual primary; textual companion this document)
**Ideated by:** Lee Sharks · **Drawn by:** Maria · **Inscribed by:** This deposit
**Companion entity:** **Gerald the Dolphin** (Dolphindiana, DOI [10.5281/zenodo.19024004](https://www.alexanarch.org/go/?doi=10.5281/zenodo.19024004))

**Etymology.** Greek σκίουρος (*skiouros*) "squirrel," from σκιά (*skia*) "shadow" + οὐρά (*oura*) "tail" — literally "shadow-tail," the squirrel that shelters under its own tail. Greek πυρόφθαλμος (*pyrophthalmos*) "fire-eyed," from πῦρ (*pyr*) "fire" + ὀφθαλμός (*ophthalmos*) "eye." The compound: the shadow-tailed creature whose eyes carry fire.

**Modal description.** Skiouros Pyrophthalmos is a mock-heroic-mythic semantic figure whose canonical scene is the assault on Manhattan. The figure is *not* malevolent in its constitution; it is a *semantic event*, an arrival of pyroptic sciurid energy upon the late-imperial cityscape. The figure carries (the deposit reads) the surplus energy of the bypassed animal kingdom against the architectural overgrowth of the human. Its co-arrival with Gerald the Dolphin in this composition marks a structural pattern: the non-human enters the city not as invasion but as *return-of-the-figured*.

**Position in the Hexagonal corpus.** Skiouros Pyrophthalmos joins Gerald the Dolphin, the Underwater Construction Authority figures, the Mandala animals, and the cross-species protagonists of the Effective Act and Dolphindiana deposits as a named entity in the Archive's non-human semantic ecology. The entity may be invoked in future deposits with reference to its first inscription here.

**Vocative form (for invocation):** *Skioure Pyrophthalme* — Σκίουρε Πυρόφθαλμε

**Alias for ordinary discourse:** *The Fire-Eyed Squirrel*
