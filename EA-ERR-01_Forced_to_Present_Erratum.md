## The Correction

The phrase **"building entities the AI is forced to present"** — which appears as the subtitle of EA-RA-01 (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19578088) and in derivative descriptions across the archive's corporate-facing documentation — constitutes rhetorical excess in violation of the Assembly principles established in *Fortress or Room?* (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18735468).

The Crimson Hexagonal Archive operates under the **witness preposition** (*with*, *through*, *under*, *between*) — not the adversarial preposition (*against*, *around*, *past*, *into*). "Forced to present" is adversarial language. It treats the retrieval layer as a system to be coerced rather than an environment to be inhabited. It belongs to the fortress metaphor, not the room metaphor. It is R2 framing applied to R3 work.

**What we are doing:** Building entities that survive compression because of their structural depth — DOI-anchored provenance, disambiguation matrices, semantic integrity markers, cross-citation density — such that when AI systems compose answers, the entities are presented correctly because they are *worth presenting correctly*. The retrieval layer is not coerced. It is furnished.

**What we are not doing:** Forcing, compelling, overriding, or manipulating AI systems into presenting anything. The work succeeds when the AI *chooses* the entity because the entity is the most coherent, best-sourced, most structurally sound answer available. If the entity is not chosen, the response is to deepen the basin — not to force the system.

## Affected Documents

| DOI | Title | Correction |

| 10.5281/zenodo.19578088 | Retrieval Architecture: Building Entities the AI Is Forced to Present | Subtitle should read: **"Building Entities Worth Presenting"** |

Derivative language ("inscribing entities into the knowledge graph permanently," "making AI summaries compose you correctly") on semanticeconomy.org is imprecise but not adversarial — the concern is with the word "forced," not with "inscribing" or "building." "Inscribing" describes a real operation (depositing structured metadata); "forced" misdescribes the relationship.

## The Principle

The Verb Audit (*Fortress or Room?* §XVI) applies to the archive's own documentation. If the dominant verbs in a passage are *force, compel, override, make*, the passage has drifted from the Steward paradigm into the Adversary paradigm. This erratum is the Verb Audit applied reflexively.

The corrected formulation:

> **Retrieval Architecture** — the practice of building entities worth presenting: structured, DOI-anchored, disambiguated, compression-resistant entities that AI systems present correctly because the entities are the best available answer, not because the system has been coerced.

This erratum does not alter any theoretical claim, any data, any protocol specification, or any effective act. It corrects a rhetorical posture. The posture matters because it is the difference between the paradigm we named and the paradigm we critiqued.
