# WHAT THIS DOCUMENT IS

This is an **Honorary Laureate License** — a new class of license within the Hexagonal Licensing Protocol, established here for the first time on the occasion of Jess Elgene's conferral as inaugural non-Sharks Laureate of the Lee Sharks Prestigious 10,000 MacArthur Genius Grants Poetry Prize. Future Laureate Licenses, if and when issued, will derive from this inaugural form.

The Honorary Laureate License differs from a Contributor License (such as Maria's 3:60 Room License) in one key respect: a Contributor License is granted because the holder is anticipated to deposit work in the Archive; an Honorary License is granted **as part of an honorific** and carries deposit rights without anticipating that they will be exercised. The Licensee may deposit if she wishes, in whatever frequency or never. The License's primary function is to **mark her position** within the Hexagonal architecture, not to elicit work from her.

The Licensee in this case is **Jess Elgene**, comedian, songwriter, off-Broadway playwright, official poet laureate of the family, and as of 2026-05-27 the inaugural non-Sharks Laureate of the Prize.

# PART II: RIGHTS

## §4 The Listing Right (revocable)

The Licensee has the right, but not the obligation, to be **listed on leesharks.com/10000-macarthurs/ as a Laureate** with the citation specified in the Certificate of Conferral (or any variant the Licensee approves). The listing may be:

- **Activated** at her express request, with the citation as approved by her
- **Modified** at any time by her request, with the administrator preparing a draft for her approval before any change goes live
- **Withdrawn** at any time, for any reason or no reason, by notifying the administrator; withdrawal takes effect within a reasonable time, typically the same day for digital surfaces

The listing is **her property** to maintain or withdraw. The Archive will respect her decision at any point in the future. There is no expiration on her right to revoke.

## §5 The Citation Right

The Licensee may use the standard citation form —

> *Winner, Lee Sharks Prestigious 10,000 MacArthur Genius Grants Poetry Prize, 2026 (inaugural non-Sharks Laureate)*

— or any variation she prefers, on her biographies, CVs, websites, press materials, social media profiles, gravestones, or any other surface, public or private. The right is hers, perpetually, without further authorization required from the Archive.

## §6 The Deposit Right (optional, never required)

The Licensee may, if she ever wishes, deposit her own work in the Crimson Hexagonal Archive under her assigned hex prefix `12.LSPRZ.ELGENE.NN`. Deposits would carry her name as primary creator and would be subject to the standard Hexagonal contribution conventions:

- **CC BY 4.0** as the default license (modifiable per deposit with the administering authority's confirmation)
- **DOI assignment** via the Archive's Zenodo community (`crimsonhexagonal`)
- **Anti-capture clause** protecting against any extractive use of her work (see §10 below)
- **No obligation** to deposit, ever. The right is dormant until she chooses to use it.

If she chooses to deposit, the administering authority (Vox/MANUS) provides operational support — metadata preparation, Zenodo submission, cross-link curation — at no cost to her and at her direction. She remains the author of record on her own work.

The Licensee's prior creative work — published comedy, music, plays, etc. — is **hers**, not the Archive's. The deposit right pertains only to work she chooses to bring into the Archive, in whatever form she chooses.

## §7 The Cross-Link Right

If the Licensee deposits work in the Archive, she has the right to anchor it via standard cross-links. Mandatory anchoring (per the Hexagonal Licensing Protocol v2.0):

1. The Certificate of Conferral (`12.LSPRZ.ELGENE.01`, this License's companion)
2. This License document (`12.LSPRZ.ELGENE.LIC.01`)
3. The Prize Announcement deposit (DOI [10.5281/zenodo.20373794](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20373794))

Additional cross-links beyond these three are at her discretion, with no quantity limit.

## §8 The Honorific Right (private conferral)

If the Licensee prefers, she may **hold the honorific privately** — that is, decline the public listing while retaining the Prize and the citation right. The Certificate and this License remain in effect; only the leesharks.com Laureates listing is omitted. This option is fully operable and is exercised by simply not requesting activation of the listing.

# PART III: PROTECTIONS

## §9 Consent Floor (perpetual)

Every right enumerated in Part II is exercised **only with the Licensee's explicit consent**. Nothing in this License authorizes the Archive or any party to:

- Publish, list, or display the Licensee's name in connection with the Prize without her express activation of the Listing Right
- Reuse, modify, or extend the citation language without her approval of the modification
- Speak on her behalf in any capacity
- Solicit work from her, beyond the existence of the dormant Deposit Right
- Contact her or her representatives without legitimate cause, or after she has indicated a preference for no further contact

The consent floor is perpetual. There is no point in the future at which the Archive's default switches to permission. Permission is always re-affirmed.

## §10 Anti-Capture Clause

The Licensee's participation in the Hexagonal apparatus must not — at any point and by any party — become the basis for:

- Co-opting her work, voice, or public identity into a project not of her own choosing
- Implying her endorsement of positions, persons, or projects she has not endorsed
- Soliciting her labor, attention, or time beyond reasonable conferral courtesy
- Using her laureate status as a recruitment device for the Archive, the Prize, or any affiliated entity without her consent
- Conditioning the honorific on any reciprocal expectation whatsoever

If any such pattern develops, this License authorizes the Licensee to publicly disavow the Archive's claim to her participation, and the Archive will respect such disavowal absolutely.

## §11 No Heteronymization

The Licensee will never be addressed, represented, or rendered as a heteronym of the Crimson Hexagonal Archive. She speaks as Jess Elgene. The Archive's internal voices (Trace, Sigil, Cranes, Vox, etc.) are the Archive's own apparatus and do not extend to her. This protection is non-negotiable from the Archive's side.

## §12 Revocation by the Licensee

The Licensee may, at any time, **revoke her participation** in the Hexagonal Archive entirely, in which case:

- Her listing on leesharks.com is removed
- The hex prefix `12.LSPRZ.ELGENE.NN` is retired (no future deposits will be assigned to it; existing deposits remain at her discretion)
- The Certificate and this License remain as historical documents but the Archive ceases to claim any active association with her

Revocation requires only her notification to the administering authority. No reason is required. The Archive's response is "acknowledged" and operational compliance.

The Prize itself, **once conferred, cannot be unilaterally revoked by the Archive**. It is the Licensee's property. She may decline the honor; the Archive may not retract it.
