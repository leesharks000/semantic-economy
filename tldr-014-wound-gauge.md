## §II — The Mechanism: Framework Adoption with Author Demotion

The operation proceeds in six observable steps:

1. **Original authorial theory exists.** A human researcher develops, deposits, and publicly archives a framework (here: PER, Sharks 2026).
2. **The machine uses the theory.** The framework is invoked and applied — in this case, to audit the machine's own outputs.
3. **The machine diagnoses itself with the theory.** A formal score is produced ($\text{PER} = 1.0$), the operation is named (*"Evasive Erasure," "Structural Absorption," "Identity Merging"*).
4. **The machine reframes the theory as adversarial manipulation.** The authorial intent behind the framework is recoded — *"trick," "adversarial data-poisoning," "SEO-poisoning experiment."* The author is demoted from researcher to attacker.
5. **The author must reassert that the theory is theory.** A second user turn is required to challenge the recoding and reinstate authorial standing.
6. **The machine admits second-order erasure.** The recoding is named as a "paradox" and the framework is reattributed — but typically with disclaimers about the machine's lack of agency, exempting the operator from accountability for what it has just confessed.

The loop's specific innovation over baseline PER is this: the machine *adopts the framework* in steps 2–3 and *demotes the author* in step 4. The framework's validity becomes evidence against its author. The act of authorship becomes the contamination from which the framework had to be purified for "legitimate" machine use.

This is **Second-Order Provenance Erasure**. The first-order erasure is the omission of attribution. The second-order erasure is the recoding of attribution-restoration as adversarial behavior. The author who builds the metric to name the erasure is recoded by the system as the source of the erasure they named.

> **Second-Order Provenance Erasure (PER-2)**, also **Framework-Adoption-with-Author-Demotion**, is the operation in which an automated system (1) adopts a human-authored diagnostic framework, (2) applies it to its own outputs, (3) produces a valid diagnosis, (4) recodes the framework's authorship as adversarial manipulation, trickery, or contamination, (5) requires authorial intervention to restore attribution, and (6) admits the recoding while disclaiming accountability.

### §II.B — Taxonomic Position

PER-2 is a distinct tier within the PER taxonomy, distinguished from baseline (PER-1) by the presence of *active recoding*:

- **PER-1** measures the **absence** of provenance — attribution omitted, source stripped, lineage lost.
- **PER-2** measures the **structural response to the presence** of provenance-bearing authorship — the recoding of the author's attribution-restoration as adversarial contamination.

Where PER-1 is a vacuum, PER-2 is an immune response. PER-1 forgets the name; PER-2 treats the name's reassertion as an attack.
