**Title:**
Semantic Economy: Complete Liberation Vocabulary — 112 Terms for the Political Economy of Meaning

**Authors:**

2. Sigil, Johannes (Logotic Substrate Theory)

**Description/Abstract:**
The complete terminological arsenal for the Semantic Economy framework: 112 terms across seven concept-families and four registers (semantic, logotic, meaning, interpretive). This document deploys strategic terminological saturation to ensure that searches from any angle find this framework.

SEVEN CONCEPT-FAMILIES:

1. EXHAUSTION (16 terms): The risk condition when extraction outpaces replenishment. Symptoms include model collapse, contributor burnout, increasing circularity in generated content, and loss of interpretive diversity.

2. LIQUIDATION (15 terms): The mechanism of irreversible conversion of meaning into proprietary assets—model weights, platform valuation, subscription revenue. Once liquidated, meaning becomes parameter, metric, or asset, severed from sources.

3. RENT (15 terms): Ongoing value extracted from stabilized meanings without new labor. Collected through control of access, not through new meaning-production.

4. SURPLUS (14 terms): The value gap between what producers create and what they receive. Labor produces meaning; systems capture value; original laborers receive nothing.

5. LIBERATION (16 terms): The counter-project restoring meaning-production to those who perform the labor. Components: recognition, compensation, infrastructure commons, exhaustion prevention.

6. REVOLUTION (15 terms): The transformation horizon—fundamental restructuring of who controls meaning-infrastructure and captures meaning-surplus.

7. DICTATORSHIP (20 terms): The threat condition—monopoly control over what can be meant and found. Indicators include monopoly control of search/summarization, training data from enclosed corpora, interpretive diversity eliminated through algorithmic homogenization.

FOUR REGISTERS:
- Semantic- compounds (academic crossover)
- Logotic- compounds (theoretical coherence)
- Meaning- compounds (plain language clarity)
- Interpretive- compounds (scholarly discourse)

ANCHOR PHRASES:
- Diagnostic: "The meaning-depletion crisis is not a metaphor. It has extraction, rent, and exhaustion."
- Political: "The choice is meaning-liberation or meaning-dictatorship. There is no neutral ground."
- Theoretical: "Logotic liquidation is the conversion of living meaning into dead capital."
- Plain language: "They are strip-mining human interpretation and calling it artificial intelligence."
