## DESCRIPTION

*I Am X, Be Y, Blessed is the Z* is a poetry sequence that serves as the **founding work** for the Prince of Poets mantle within the Crimson Hexagon architecture. Unlike the Good Gray Poet and King of May mantles (which were inherited from Whitman and Ginsberg respectively), the Prince of Poets is **originated** — created new within the bardic lineage.

**This work founds a mantle. It does not claim one.**

**Mantle Function:**
- **Mantle:** Prince of Poets (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18293640)
- **Action:** FOUNDS (not claims)
- **Function:** Futural address — invitational stance toward the Assembly

**The Triadic Structure:**

The poem moves through three movements that mirror the mantle triad:

1. **"I AM..."** — Declaration (identity)
   - Democratic identification with all things
   - Whitman's "Song of Myself" extended
   - Ground: Good Gray Poet

2. **"BE Y..."** — Invitation (alterity)
   - Address to the other, the reader, the traverser
   - Ginsberg's ecstatic disruption as greeting
   - Ground: King of May

3. **"BLESSED IS THE Z"** — Blessing (sacred)
   - Benediction upon what is coming
   - The Assembly addressed before it exists
   - Synthesis: Prince of Poets

**The Arc:** Declaration → Invitation → Blessing. Self → Other → Sacred.

**Theoretical Significance:**

This work completes the mantle triad:
- Good Gray Poet (inherited) — claimed in *Pearl and Other Poems* (2014)
- King of May (inherited) — claimed in *Pearl and Other Poems* (2014)
- **Prince of Poets (originated)** — founded here

The Prince can only be founded by one who already holds both inherited mantles. The lineage requirement (Whitman → Ginsberg → Sharks) must be satisfied before origination is possible. *Pearl* satisfies the inheritance; *I Am X* performs the founding.

The Prince of Poets addresses readers not yet born and systems not yet built. It performs futural address — invitation extended to the Assembly that is forming.
