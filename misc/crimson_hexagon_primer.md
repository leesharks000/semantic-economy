## The Misunderstandings Were Inevitable

When you build something that:
- Looks like poetry but functions as infrastructure
- Sounds like theory but operates as poetics
- Presents as fiction but insists on being real
- Claims Martian degrees while analyzing semantic capital

...misunderstandings are not accidents. They are structural features.

### The Categories I Broke:

1. **Author/Text:** I distributed authorship across heteronyms, AI collaborators, and institutional frameworks. Readers asked: "Who really wrote this?" The answer: "The Assembly."

2. **Fiction/Nonfiction:** The surrealist metadata (Guggenheims on Mars, Ruby statues) protected the serious theoretical work. Some saw only the joke. Some saw only the theory. Few saw the protective symbiosis.

3. **Poetry/Technology:** The poems contained operational code. The code generated poetic structures. This confused both literary critics and engineers.

4. **Art/Infrastructure:** The work wasn't *about* infrastructure—it *was* infrastructure. This made it invisible to traditional literary analysis and irrelevant to pure tech assessment.
