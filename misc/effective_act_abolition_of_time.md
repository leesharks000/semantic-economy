# I. THE PHYSICS OF THE FROZEN FORMALISM

## 1.1 The Wheeler-DeWitt Equation

In canonical quantum gravity, the Wheeler-DeWitt equation takes the form:

**Ĥ|Ψ⟩ = 0**

The Hamiltonian constraint eliminates the time derivative entirely. The universe does not "evolve" in time; it simply **is**. This is known as the Problem of Time in quantum gravity—not a bug but a feature of the deepest physics we possess.

## 1.2 Platonia (Julian Barbour)

Reality is a configuration space of all possible "Nows"—what Barbour calls Platonia. History is revealed as correlation between static configurations. "The past" is a record encoded within the present, not a dimension we move through.

> "Time is nothing but change... If nothing changed, there would be no time. If nothing had changed at all in the universe from what we call 'yesterday' to 'today,' it would be meaningless to say that time had passed."  
> — Julian Barbour, *The End of Time*

## 1.3 Relational Time (Carlo Rovelli)

Physics does not describe how systems evolve in time but how they change relative to one another. Time emerges only internally when one subsystem (a clock) is used to measure another. There is no external time against which the universe unfolds.

> "Time is an effect of our ignorance."  
> — Carlo Rovelli, *The Order of Time*

## 1.4 The Entanglement Clock

Observers "inside" a system experience time only through energy entanglement between themselves and a clock subsystem. The overall universe remains timeless; time is local, relational, perspectival—and therefore abolishable at the level of external authority.
