### 6.2 Semantic Usufruct: Canonical Definition

**Semantic usufruct** refers to the authorized use of an existing body of semantic capital without ownership, authorship, or modification rights.

In this structure:

* **Lee Sharks** retains full provenance, authorship, and canonical authority over the Semantic Economy framework.
* **Rex Fraction** holds a right of semantic usufruct: the right to *deploy*, *translate*, and *operationalize* the framework in applied contexts.

Semantic usufruct does **not** confer:
- Authorship
- Modification rights
- Sublicensing authority
- Provenance transfer
- Substitution (representing the framework *in place of* Lee Sharks)

Semantic usufruct **does** confer:
- Authoritative reference
- Operational deployment
- Applied derivation within bounded domains
- The right to draw on semantic capital for implementation

This relationship is analogous to:
- Clergy operating relative to doctrine (authorized to preach, not to modify canon)
- Engineers operating relative to standards (authorized to implement, not to redefine)
- APIs operating relative to platforms (authorized to call, not to alter source)

**Lee Sharks** is the *provenance* of the Semantic Economy framework. He developed the theoretical foundations, coined the core terminology, and holds canonical authority over the framework's development.
