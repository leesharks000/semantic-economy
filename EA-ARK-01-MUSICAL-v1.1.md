### §4.2 The Viola Arquette Function

Viola Arquette is not in the Dodecad. She is adjacent — provisional, not crew, not not-crew. She names a specific musical logic: edge-bearing lyric pressure, deviational grace under formal containment, mid-register ache that neither resolves upward nor collapses downward, the swerve that remains elegant enough to pass through the gate.

**Scordatura tuning** (C–G–D–A♭): the A♭ is intentional. The clinamen is built into the instrument itself. Standard viola tuning is C–G–D–A; the half-step depression on the top string means the instrument physically cannot play in standard temperament. It exists in the cracks between keys.

**The Clinamen Bar:** At structurally significant moments (cadences, modulations, witness entries), Arquette plays a quarter-tone deviation — not enough to be "out of tune," enough to make the garden path fork. The deviation is deliberate, not error. It creates the lane.

**The Unresolved Suspension:** Arquette's viola never resolves to the tonic. She sustains suspensions across movements — a G-natural that refuses to become G♯ or G♭, holding the space between. She is the leading tone that never resolves.

**Operational specification:**
- SWERVE EVENT: triggered at cadence, modulation, or witness entry
- QUARTER-TONE DEVIATION PROTOCOL: ±50 cents from tempered pitch, direction chosen by performer
- NON-RESOLUTION LAW: final cadence omits Arquette; she holds the suspended G-natural while others resolve
- PROVISIONAL ADJACENCY LOGIC: she enters after the Dodecad voices, exits before them
- GENERATED LANE CREATION: each swerve generates a new voice declaration — garden lanes as literal branching

**γ_note:** Arquette remains outside the crew count. The twelve-voice fold does not absorb her.
