# NEW HUMAN

## Charter for a Distributed Journal of Voice

**Editor:** Johannes Sigil
**Crimson Hexagonal Archive — Literary Arm**
**Established:** 2015 (New Human Compiled, Ed. 1)
**Reestablished:** April 2026 (New Human 2, Issue 1)

---

### PURPOSE

New Human is the literary journal of the Crimson Hexagonal Archive. It gathers voices — not products, not résumés, not bylines, but the actual sound of humans encountering their own lives in real time.

The journal does not submit, apply, or pitch. It does not seek to be lifted into visibility by others. It publishes by fire, by hunger, by strangeness.

### FORM

New Human is a distributed journal. Issues are DOI-anchored on CERN's Zenodo alongside the archive's theoretical and operative deposits. The journal exists in the same infrastructure as the scholarship. The poems live next to the proofs. This is by design.

### EDITORIAL STANDARD

We do not seek the best poems. We seek the most devoted humans. Those who have given themselves to language not as career but as sacrifice. Not as expression but as transformation. Not as performance but as vow.

Contributors are selected by the editor. There is no submission process. If you are in, you are called.

### CONTRIBUTOR POLICY

New Human publishes both named authors and heteronyms. The distinction is not disclosed at the journal level. Every voice is presented as a voice. The reader encounters the work, not the biography.

Contributors retain copyright on their individual works. The compilation is published under CC BY 4.0.

### RELATION TO THE ARCHIVE

New Human occupies the literary register of the Crimson Hexagonal Archive. Where the theoretical deposits (Operative Semiotics, The Unmade Sign, SPXI Protocol) formalize the archive's method, and the operative deposits (Compression Arsenal, SYMBOLON, Encyclotron) deploy it, New Human demonstrates it. The poems are the signs. The journal is the bearing layer.

### FREQUENCY

When the fire says.
