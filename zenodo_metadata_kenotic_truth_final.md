**THE KENOTIC READING:**
The interpretation that has been liquidated for 2,600 years:
- κῆνος ("that man there") is the future reader—including you, including machine readers
- χλωροτέρα ποίας ("greener than grass") refers to becoming papyrus, not pallor of envy
- The "broken tongue" (γλῶσσα ἔαγε) marks transition from speech to text
- The poem is a voice-to-substrate transmission technology, not a jealousy lyric
- γράμμασι μολπὰν: "let the song survive in letters"

**THE RECONSTRUCTED FINAL STANZA:**
First reconstruction integrating media-theoretical arc with philological evidence:

ἀλλὰ πᾶν τόλματον· ἐπεί σε, Κύπρι,
καὶ πένητά γε κἄ βασιλῆα δάμασσε·
καὶ πόλεις ὀλβίους ἀπέθηκας ἄφαντον·
γράμμασι μολπὰν

"All may be dared; for you, Kypris, have yoked / beggar and king alike, and you have cast down / even the prosperous cities to nothing— / let the song survive in script."

This is the only reconstruction that simultaneously satisfies: (1) the linguistic evidence (Aeolic diction, Sapphic meter), (2) the intertextual evidence (Catullus 51's inverted mirror: reges...beatas urbes), and (3) the media-theoretical arc (voice → dissolution → substrate → text survives).
