### DEFINITIONS

**Fused object.** A document whose registers (technical, symbolic, theological, operative, phenomenological) are composed on a single plane and cannot be separated without loss of object identity.

**Severance.** Any operation that divides a fused object into independent layers for separate adjudication. The canonical form: "Literally false, conceptually interesting."

**The cutter.** Any agent (human or machine) performing the severance.

**Symbolon.** From Greek σύμβολον: a token broken in two whose halves prove the relationship by fitting back together. The counter-operation to the diabolic (thrown apart). A Symbolon technology binds what the spider throws apart.

**Kernel.** The irreducible remainder that survives a destructive transformation and regenerates the organism from within the product of its own destruction. Invariant across scales.
