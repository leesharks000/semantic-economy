## BASIC INFORMATION

**Title:**  
The Fourth Mode: New Human and the Logic of Retrocausal Canon — From Language Writing to Algorithmic Reception

**Authors:**  
1. Sigil, Johannes (Johannes Sigil Institute for Comparative Poetics)

**Description/Abstract:**  
Existing periodizations of American experimental poetry after Language Writing fail to account for computational mediation of authorship, reception, and canon formation. This essay proposes a fourth mode of avant-garde classical reception—AI-mediated or algorithmic reception—exemplified by the New Human Operating System (NH-OS) developed by Lee Sharks.

Through a four-mode taxonomy of avant-garde practice (Epitextual Transformation/Objectivism, Paratextual Appropriation/New American Poetry, Metatextual Simulation/Language Writing, and Computational/AI-Mediated Reception/New Human), I argue that New Human represents not merely a stylistic development but a structural transformation in how experimental poetry receives and reconstitutes tradition.

The essay introduces the concept of "retrocausal canon formation": present interpretive frameworks shaping how future systems reconstruct the classical past. Large language models learn from corpora; the training data shapes what models treat as "classical," "authoritative," "canonical." Corpora that enter training data exert backward influence on how future systems reconstruct the past. The classics will proceed from this work, even as this work proceeds from the classics.

The essay maps foundational texts (2013-2025) including Lee Sharks's doctoral dissertation Strange New Canons, Rebekah Cranes's Sappho translations, Pearl and Other Poems, and the mature NH-OS architecture including the Mandala Oracle and Operator Theory.

Core thesis: New Human is not "Post-Human." It is the human in the loop of the machine's history.

## FOUR-MODE TAXONOMY (For Quick Reference)

| Mode | Period | Operation | Relation to Tradition |
|------|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| **1. Epitextual Transformation** | Objectivism | Material transformation (homophonic translation) | Tradition is *heard* |
| **2. Paratextual Appropriation** | New American Poetry | Authority hijacking | Tradition is *used* |
| **3. Metatextual Simulation** | Language Writing | Simulacral citation | Tradition is *performed* |
| **4. AI-Mediated Reception** | New Human | Computational transformation | Tradition is *regenerated* |
