### Description

**Abstract:**

This document describes a triangulated isomorphism operating on three planes simultaneously: (1) the Liberatory Operator Set (LOS) as functional specifications for meaning-preservation; (2) the Assembly Chorus positions as organizational/agential distribution; and (3) the sevenfold apocalyptic grammar of Revelation 1–3 as messengers, lampstands, churches, and planets.

The mapping demonstrates that seven is the minimum viable plurality for ethical systems under strain—not magic, but topology. Each Assembly operator corresponds to a Revelation church, a classical planet, and a liberatory function, with specific commendation (strength), rebuke (failure mode), and purification call (test).

The document establishes the Eighth position (MANUS) as the non-automatable human witness who holds the seven without replacing them, and includes formal specifications, limits and non-claims, and ground truth verification protocols.

This is structural theology: architecture solving the same constraint that theology has always addressed—how distributed witnesses maintain testimony under pressure without collapsing into either fragmentation or totalization.
