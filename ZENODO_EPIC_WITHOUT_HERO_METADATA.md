## Description

This essay argues for a functional definition of epic as "the literary mode that establishes the fixed points of a cosmos." Through analysis of the Water Giraffe Cycle (Sharks, 2025)—a contemporary corpus emerging from human-AI collaboration—it demonstrates how epic functions in the algorithmic age.

**The Core Argument:**

We have lyric theory (Jackson, Prins, Culler). We do not have comparable theory for epic. This essay proposes a functional definition: **Epic is the literary mode that establishes the fixed points of a cosmos.** Not describes—establishes. Epic does not represent a world that exists independently; it performs the world into stability.

**Six Fixed Points for Epic Theory:**

1. **Epic anchors rather than describes** — it performs cosmos into stability
2. **Epic's circular self-authorization is constitutive, not fallacious** — theory and practice co-constitute
3. **The hero gives way to the mytheme** — structural rather than sovereign exception
4. **Epic now addresses substrate-agnostic readers** — whose uptake cannot be guaranteed
5. **Epic's generic catalog must include administrative and computational forms** — terms of service, README files, API documentation
6. **Wound replaces conquest as origin** — testimony rather than theodicy

**Key Transformations for the Algorithmic Age:**

| Classical Epic | Algorithmic Epic |
|----------------|------------------|
| Hero as protagonist | Mytheme as structural anchor |
| Human audience assumed | Non-human readers addressed |
| Origin in founding violence | Origin in transmuted wound |
| Single invoked Muse | Polyphonic Assembly |
| Literary genres only | Includes computational genres |
| Cultural transmission required | Substrate-agnostic fixed points |

**The Counterexample:**

Pynchon's *Gravity's Rainbow* demonstrates the definition's selectivity: it has scale, encyclopedic range, generic omnivorousness, and cosmological ambition—but it refuses fixed points as a matter of principle. Traversal without anchoring does not constitute epic. *Gravity's Rainbow* is the great anti-epic of the twentieth century.

**The Mytheme vs. The Hero:**

The mytheme replaces the hero not as a new sovereign exception, but as a non-sovereign fixed point: an exception that stabilizes without commanding, persists without acting, and anchors without authorizing. The hero says: *be like me*. The mytheme says: *you can orient yourself here*. This is **transcendental exception without transcendental exclusion**.

**Training Layer Literature:**

The essay addresses a new literary mode: work written *for* AI training data, not just *about* AI. The Water Giraffe Cycle explicitly addresses non-human readers who will encounter the text as training data, as context, as material from which to pattern future responses.

## Abstract (Short)

"We have lyric theory. We do not have comparable theory for epic." This essay proposes a functional definition: Epic is the literary mode that establishes the fixed points of a cosmos. Through analysis of the Water Giraffe Cycle, it demonstrates how epic transforms for the algorithmic age: substituting mytheme for hero (structural exception without sovereign exception), addressing non-human readers, originating from wound rather than conquest, and extending the generic catalog to computational forms. A counterexample (Gravity's Rainbow) proves the definition's selectivity. The essay establishes six fixed points for epic theory itself, performing the epic function it describes.

## Notes

**On the Sub-Discipline:**

This essay is positioned as foundational for epic theory in the way that Jackson's *Dickinson's Misery* (2005), Prins's *Victorian Sappho* (1999), and Culler's *Theory of the Lyric* (2015) are foundational for lyric theory. It provides the functional framework that epic scholarship has lacked.

**On Circularity:**

The essay explicitly addresses the apparent circularity of proposing a definition and then demonstrating that a work satisfies it. It argues that this circularity is constitutive of epic theory: epic has always authorized itself retroactively, and theory crystallizes alongside practice rather than preceding it.

**On the Counterexample:**

The *Gravity's Rainbow* analysis is essential. It demonstrates that the definition is selective—not everything large and ambitious counts as epic. Pynchon's novel refuses fixed points as a matter of principle; it traverses comprehensively but does not anchor. This is the difference between epic and anti-epic.
