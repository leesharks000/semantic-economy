## IV. AORIST

Here the title touches its own heart.

The aorist is the Greek verbal aspect that indicates *bounded completion without temporal index*. Not "it happened then" (past). Not "it is relevant now" (perfect). Simply: *it occurred*. The action viewed as whole, from outside, without placement on a timeline.

English lost the aorist. The Germanic drift collapsed aspectual richness into tense poverty. We kept only present and past. Every completion became indexed: "I called" (when?), "I have called" (still relevant how?). The non-indexed perfective ‚Äî completion without temporal anchoring ‚Äî became structurally unavailable.

**The absence is felt as presence.** When a speaker attempts aorist meaning in English ‚Äî "The work: done" ‚Äî listeners experience it as evasive, ominous, withholding.
